Age: 18-65

Donors must be between 18 and 65 years old. 16-17 year olds may donate with parental consent in some regions.

Weight: 50kg+

You must weigh at least 50 kg (110 lbs) to donate whole blood safely without health risks.

Good Health

You should feel well on the day of donation. No colds, flu, or other infections. Normal iron levels are required.

Start Your Hero Journey

Preparation

  • Hydrate Well

    Drink an extra 500ml of water or non-alcoholic fluids before your appointment.

  • Eat a Healthy Meal

    Avoid fatty foods and focus on iron-rich options like spinach or lean meat.

  • Get Good Sleep

    Ensure a restful 7-8 hours of sleep the night before your donation.

Recovery

  • Stay for Refreshments

    Don't rush! Wait for 15 minutes and enjoy a snack to stabilize your sugar levels.

  • Keep the Bandage On

    Keep it for at least 4-5 hours to prevent bruising and promote healing.

  • Avoid Heavy Lifting

    Give your arm a rest for the remainder of the day. No intense gym sessions!

Myths vs. Facts

Myth: Donating blood hurts a lot.

Fact: You may feel a slight pinch when the needle goes in, but the process is mostly painless. Most donors say the satisfaction outweighs the discomfort.

Myth: I'll get weak or sick.

Fact: You only donate about 1 pint, which your body replaces fluid-wise within 24-48 hours. Most donors resume normal activities immediately.

Myth: I can't donate with a tattoo.

Fact: You CAN donate! Usually, you just need to wait 3-6 months after getting a tattoo if it was done at a regulated facility.
